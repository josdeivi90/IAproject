Fundamental Architectures for Intelligent Automation

Comparison of Architectural Approaches

The optimal solution integrates Intelligent Process Automation (IPA), AI agents, and Large Language Models (LLMs).

A hybrid approach is the most complete and robust to meet multifaceted requirements, enabling a system that reads, executes, reports, and learns in an integrated manner.

Core Components of the AI System

1. Document Understanding

AI reads and interprets technical documentation, extracting actions and parameters through:

IDP/OCR (Digitization)
↓
NLP/NLU (Semantic Analysis)
↓
LLM/Transformers (Deep Extraction)

Key technologies: NLU, OCR, IDP, Transformer Models, LLM.

2. Task Execution by AI Agents

Intelligent agents with dynamic planning execute procedures by interacting with software via:

Agents must be versatile and adapt to failures or changes.

3. Comprehensive Reports and Auditing

Structured logs and detailed reports of each step are generated, including:

  • Process and Step ID
  • Parameters and Results
  • Timestamps and Status
  • AI Confidence
  • Human Validation (if applicable)

These reports are crucial for transparency, debugging, and the learning cycle.

4. Continuous Learning Validated by Humans

A dual learning cycle improves the system:

Direct Correction (HITL)

Humans validate and correct specific errors. AI learns by imitation or fine-tuning.

Global Optimization (RLHF)

General validations train a Reward Model, optimizing the agent's policy in the long term.

This creates a system that not only corrects errors but also aligns its behavior with human standards.

Critical Implementation Considerations

Phased Roadmap

Phase 1: Proof of Concept (3-6m)

Validate core technical feasibility with limited scope.

Phase 2: Pilot (6-9m)

Implement HITL loop, initiate learning training.

Phase 3: Limited Production (6-12m)

Deploy in real environment, monitor, activate RLHF.

Phase 4: Scaling and Optimization (Continuous)

Expand and continuously improve the system.
